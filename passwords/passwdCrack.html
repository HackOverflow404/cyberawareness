How are Passwords Cracked?

How do Hackers get your passwords?

There are multiple ways in which a hacker can get someone’s password:

  • Brute Forcing
  • Social Engineering
  • Malware
  • Breaking into a website’s or application’s database (although the user can’t really do much about his.)

Social Engineering

Social engineering is all about tricking people and manipulating them. There are many ways to do this, but one of the most common methods is phishing. Phishing attacks are tricking a person that some sort of message is coming from a trusted source, such as amazon or google, and tricks the person into logging into a fake website or downloading some malicious file.

Example of phishing email (this example is completely made up):

Dear ShopHere customer,
We noticed that your credit card is near its expiry date, please update it within 14 days.

If you think there is a mistake, please sign into your account at www.shophere.com and call the number in the help section of your account.

Thanks & Regards
ShopHere Support

And the hacker can hide a malicious url in the "www.shophere.com" link leading to a website designed to look just like ShopHere.

Malware

There are many types of malware - adware, viruses, worms, trojans, bots, ransomware, spyware, etc. - and they can be used to obtain a person’s password.

A hacker can use social engineering or directly attack a person’s device to install malware, and then run a malicious file or script to capture a person’s password among other things.

One example of how a hacker can do this is using a keylogger. Keyloggers are a type of spyware which ‘log’ or record a person’s interactions with their device. Whether they are typing something, clicking something, or taking a screenshot, a hacker will be able to access all of it if a keylogger is installed.

Keyloggers come in two forms, physical and digital keyloggers.

Physical keyloggers, often called rubber duckies, have to be manually plugged in into a port of a device, so if you ever recognize an unknown device plugged in, it is recommended to immediately remove it.

Digital keyloggers are software based, and they have to be downloaded. They then log the same information as physical keyloggers and then transmit the information to the hacker. Digital keyloggers can be identified by antivirus applications or can be noticed in task manager or activity monitor and then uninstalled.

Brute Forcing

Brute forcing is when a hacker tries to guess a person’s password by trying out many, many combinations of characters. There are two types of brute force attacks, common passwords that are compiled together in enormous publicly available lists called password dictionaries.
