Cyber Awareness

What is cyber awareness?

Most people know that there is always a risk present when it comes to devices, but not many people know how to prevent them or fix them when they do occur.

The formal definition: cyber awareness is developing a sense of awareness and understanding of the risks and threats present regarding our devices.

The informal definition: cyber awareness is just knowing how to keep your stuff safe and what to do if it is compromised.

Why do we care about cyber awareness?

Now why should you even care about cyber awareness?

In today’s world, everything we do is, in one way or another, connected to technology.

Whether we want to contact our friends or family, or if we want to turn in our homework for a class, or if we want to send a quick email to our boss, it’s all done through our devices.

We trust these machines with much of our personal information that we wouldn’t want to share publicly.

And knowing how to protect these devices and the sensitive data they contain is crucial. I mean, you don’t want it falling into the wrong hands right?

How do hackers do what they do?

Well hackers are able to hack you, because of mistakes you make. According to Cybintsolutions.com, “95% of cybersecurity breaches are caused by human error”. One example could be an employee posting a picture of themself working at their desk on their LinkedIn profile. This may provide a hacker with numerous clues, such as what is the make and model of their computer (to try and search for vulnerabilities), or maybe what current project they are working on (to try and social engineer some information from them). To a hacker, any piece of information is valuable.
