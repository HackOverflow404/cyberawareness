How can we Protect Against Phishing Attacks?

Recognizing Phishing Attempts

Email Phishing
Be cautious of unexpected emails, especially those urging immediate action. Check for spelling errors, generic greetings, and suspicious email addresses. Verify the legitimacy of unexpected attachments or links by contacting the sender directly.

Website Phishing
Before entering personal information on a website, ensure it is secure. Look for "https://" in the URL and a padlock icon in the address bar. Avoid clicking on links in emails; instead, manually type the website's address.

Phone and SMS Phishing
Exercise caution with unsolicited calls or text messages, especially those requesting sensitive information. Legitimate organizations won't ask for sensitive details over the phone. Verify the authenticity of the request independently.

Keep Software and Systems Updated

Regularly update your operating system, antivirus software, and all applications. Security patches and updates are often released to address vulnerabilities that could be exploited by phishing attacks.

Report Suspected Phishing Attempts

If you encounter a potential phishing attempt, report it to your organization's IT department or the legitimate entity being impersonated. Reporting can help prevent others from falling victim to the same scam.

Secure Password Practices

Strong, Unique Passwords
Create strong, unique passwords for each online account. Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid easily guessable information such as birthdays or names.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Enable 2FA whenever possible. This adds an extra layer of security by requiring a secondary verification method, such as a code sent to your mobile device, in addition to your password.

Educate Yourself and Stay Informed

Security Awareness Training
Participate in security awareness training programs to learn about the latest phishing techniques and how to recognize them. Many organizations provide online resources to educate users on cybersecurity best practices.

Stay Informed About Current Threats
Stay abreast of current phishing threats by following reputable cybersecurity blogs, news sources, and official communications from your workplace or relevant organizations.

Use Email Filtering

Leverage email filtering tools to automatically detect and filter out potential phishing emails. These tools can help reduce the likelihood of malicious emails reaching your inbox.
