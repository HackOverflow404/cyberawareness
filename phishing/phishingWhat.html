What are Phishing Attacks?

How Phishing Works

Phishing is a cyber attack where deceptive methods are used to manipulate individuals into revealing their sensitive information. Attackers cast a wide net to lure potential victims, similar to a fisherman casting a net to catch fish.


There are multiple parts to a phishing attack:

  • Bait Creation: Attackers create deceptive content, often in the form of a fake email, text message, or website. This content pretends to be from a trusted source, like a bank or a popular website. It typically creates a false sense of urgency.
  • Deceptive Content: The bait contains misleading information, such as a counterfeit login page that appears identical to the real one, or a request for personal information to seem legitimate.
  • Contacting the Target: Attackers distribute the bait to numerous potential victims through email or messages. The messages often contain language meant to make the victim respond quickly.
  • Victim's Response: If the victim falls for the deception and clicks a link or provides the requested information, they have taken the bait.
  • Data Theft: Once the victim takes the bait, the attacker collects the provided information, which can include login credentials, credit card details, or personal data. This information can be misused for malicious purposes.

Phishing attacks come in various forms, including email phishing, spear phishing targeting specific individuals with personalized messages, vishing (voice phishing) via phone calls, smishing through text messages, and pharming, which redirects victims to fake websites.

Types of Phishing

Phishing attacks come in various forms. Here are some common types:

  • Email Phishing: You receive an email that appears to be from a trustworthy source, but it's actually from a cybercriminal seeking your information.
  • Spear Phishing: This type of phishing is highly targeted. Attackers research their victims and send personalized messages to trick them.
  • Vishing: Vishing is voice phishing, where attackers use phone calls to impersonate legitimate entities and request sensitive information.
  • Smishing: Smishing uses text messages to trick victims into clicking links or providing information.
  • Pharming: Pharming redirects victims to a fraudulent website even if they type the correct web address.

Example of Phishing

Here is an example of a phishing attack:


Urgent Account Verification Required

Dear Customer,

We regret to inform you that your account security has been compromised. To protect your funds and sensitive information, we kindly request you to verify your account by clicking the link below:

Click Here to Verify Your Account

Failure to verify your account within the next 48 hours will result in the suspension of your account and a freeze on all transactions. We take your security seriously and apologize for any inconvenience this may cause.

Thank you for your immediate attention to this matter.

Sincerely, Your Bank Support Team
