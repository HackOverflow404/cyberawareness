What are Phishing Attacks?

How Phishing Works

Phishing is a form of cyber attack where deceptive methods are used to manipulate individuals into revealing their sensitive information. The term "phishing" is derived from "fishing," as attackers cast a wide net to lure potential victims, similar to a fisherman casting a net to catch fish.

There are multiple parts to a phishing attack:

  • Bait Creation: Attackers create deceptive content, often in the form of a fake email, text message, or website. This content pretends to be from a trusted source, like a bank or a popular website. It typically creates a false sense of urgency.
  • Deceptive Content: The bait contains misleading information.   • Contacting the Target: Attackers distribute the bait to numerous potential victims through email or messages. The messages often contain language meant to make the victim respond quickly.
  • Victim's Response: If the victim falls for the deception and clicks a link or provides the requested information, they have taken the bait.
  • Data Theft: Once the victim takes the bait, the attacker collects the provided information, which can include login credentials, credit card details, or personal data. This information can be misused for malicious purposes.

Phishing attacks come in various forms, including email phishing, which involves deceptive emails, spear phishing targeting specific individuals with personalized messages, vishing (voice phishing) via phone calls, smishing through text messages, and pharming, which redirects victims to fake websites.

To protect yourself from phishing attacks, it's essential to verify the sender's authenticity, scrutinize the content for errors, double-check URLs before clicking, be cautious of urgent or too-good-to-be-true messages, and enable two-factor authentication when possible. Keeping your software updated and using antivirus programs can also help detect and prevent phishing attempts. Reporting any phishing messages you receive contributes to efforts to combat this form of cybercrime.
